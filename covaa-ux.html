Questions
Q1 · Pick 2
What should CoVAA signal to teachers?
"Safe space to reflect" leads at 6/10. "Help you improve" and "Practical professional feedback" follow at 5/10 each.
The two top messages scored equally. The product may need to hold both rather than choose one.
Q2 · Pick 3
How should it come across to teachers?
Supportive 9/10 and Credible 8/10 — near-unanimous. Approachable vs. Professional is where the groups split.
Approachable: 5 product team, 0 AST. Professional: 3 AST, 1 product team. The core tension is visible here.
Q3 · Pick 2
Which tones feel least suitable?
Authoritative — 7/10. Both groups agree it must not feel top-down.
Corporate was mainly flagged by the product team. Casual was mainly flagged by AST.

Q8 · Pick 3
Teachers are most wary if it feels…
"Feels like judging them" — 9/10. "Like extra admin work" and "Hard to use" — both 8/10. Non-negotiable.
Ease of use (80%) is near-universal too — it signals trust, not just convenience.
Q9 · Pick any
What's most important to improve for teachers?
"Make the support easier to understand" leads at 6/10. "Make it feel more useful to return to" follows at 5/10.
Product team focused on stickiness. AST focused on clarity and trust. Both priorities are close.

01
We want CoVAA to feel like a warm mentor — supportive and credible.
Supportive · 9/10 Credible · 8/10 Not judging them · 9/10
Supportive 9/10 · Credible 8/10 · "Feels like judging them" 9/10 · "Safe space to reflect" 6/10 · "Help you improve" 5/10
02
We don't want CoVAA to make teachers feel judged, or burdened with extra work.
If it feels like an evaluation, teachers disengage. If it feels like more on top of an already full day, they stop opening it. Either one is enough to lose them.
"Feels like judging them" — 9/10 · "Like extra admin work" — 8/10 · "Hard to use" — 8/10
03
We want the value of CoVAA to be quickly obvious to teachers.
Teachers won't give it a second chance if the first one doesn't land. CoVAA should answer "what's in it for me?" before anyone has to ask.
"Support easier to understand" #1 fix at 6/10 · "Worth returning to" 5/10 · "Clearer what it's for" and "Clearer how it's different" also flagged

"
Can consider behavioural insights EAST framework (Easy, Attractive, Social, and Timely) to increase adoption rates. E.g. tease and engage with scarcity — "You have only 3 tokens. Choose 3 areas you want the feedback to be focused on. Come back next week for another 3 tokens." Or language learning app style — timely email reminders to focus on one learning point this week, the next learning point the following week. Idea is to make feedback application manageable in bite sizes for tiny tastes of success that will bring the user back because use of the app brought actual results for them. Many more ideas in the EAST framework to explore!
