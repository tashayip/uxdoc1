Changelog
Recent mission creation updates.
v31 20 Apr 2026 Latest
Form layout reverted to v22 style — single card per step, header inside
The v30 split-card layout stretched the form out vertically and made it harder to read. Reverting to v22's pattern (one section-card per step, step header inside the card, field-cluster dividers within) restores the cleaner rhythm without removing any fields.
Buddy section redesigned as a browsable character picker
Previously the Buddy section showed a 3-card role selector that immediately opened a form. Now teachers browse a richer grid of 6 preset characters — each showing name, personality snippet, voice, and a preview button — and only reveal the editing form by clicking "Refine character".
Six preset Buddy characters: Lin Laoshi, Kai, Coach Ravi, Story Mei, Nadia, Custom
Three roles (Teacher / Friend / Custom) were too few to capture teaching styles. Six named presets with distinct personalities and voices give teachers more meaningful choice without requiring them to configure everything from scratch.
Voice preview added directly to each buddy card
Teachers can audition any voice before selecting, without opening the refine panel. Each card's "Preview" button speaks a short greeting in the selected MTL language so the voice is immediately evaluable in context.
Form width constrained and centred
Without a max-width the form stretched edge-to-edge on wide screens, making long text fields very hard to read. A page-mid wrapper caps the content at 800px and centres it.
Gender reduced to Female / Male (Neutral removed)
The Neutral option was retained from earlier iterations but has no corresponding voice or persona distinction in the current preset set. Removed to simplify the choice.
"More options" moved to bottom of Step 2 card
Previously inlined mid-card, which made the optional fields feel like required ones. Placing it at the very bottom signals that these are truly optional overrides.
Step 2 cluster renamed "Instruct AI to carry out the mission" — tagged 🚫 Hidden from students
"What the Buddy is told to do" was vague and teacher-unfamiliar. The new label makes the purpose explicit (these fields are AI instructions) and the badge makes the audience immediately clear.
Goal order compressed to two inline radio buttons
The previous pill group took up a full row and required a label above it. Two inline radio buttons occupy a single line and are self-labelling, reducing vertical height with no loss of clarity.
"What students will see" renamed to "Describe a mission students will like"
The old label described what the field was; the new label is a direct instruction that primes teachers to write in a student-motivating register.
"Resources" → "Customise data sources"; "Language settings" → "Set language settings"
More descriptive and action-oriented labels that clarify what each cluster does at a glance.
Full-bleed separator rule between clusters
A label underline was too subtle to signal a section boundary. A full-width rule bleeding edge-to-edge across the card padding creates a clear, scannable boundary without adding extra whitespace.
Text hierarchy: step titles increased to 22px H1; cluster sub-headings restyled to 13px H2
At 18px the step title was only slightly larger than the 10.5px all-caps cluster labels, making the hierarchy hard to read. Increasing the step title to 22px and converting cluster labels from tiny uppercase to 13px mixed-case creates a proper two-level heading hierarchy within each card.
v30 20 Apr 2026
Form restructured — step headers pulled outside cards (Stripe / Linear pattern)
Step titles sitting inside the card they describe created visual noise and competed with field labels. Moving each numbered step header outside and above its card(s) gives the form a cleaner vertical rhythm and makes the step structure obvious before the teacher reads a single field.
Each step split into thematic cards
Steps 1–3 previously crammed all fields into one wide card. Splitting by category (e.g. setup vs. student-facing, AI task vs. resources, character vs. language settings) gives each topic its own breathing room and lets teachers scan and skip to the card they care about.
Buddy section flattened — character-selection-card nesting removed
The role-grid and customise panel were nested three containers deep, producing an extra inset and inconsistent spacing. Both now sit as direct siblings inside the step card, aligned to the same 32px padding as every other field in the form.
Buddy focus checklist removed
The checklist had no clear mapping to the prompt spec and was adding cognitive overhead. Removed for now — explicit focus behaviours live in Extra notes or in the Buddy preset defaults.
"Coaching notes" renamed to "Extra notes"
"Coaching notes" implied instructions for a human coach. "Extra notes" is a plain, open-ended label that invites any kind of override or qualifier without prescribing the form it should take.
Step navigation updated to show/hide step-groups
goToStep() and showReview() now toggle visibility on .step-group wrappers instead of individual sec-N cards, ensuring all cards within a step appear and disappear together.
v29 20 Apr 2026
Field hints moved above inputs, between label and field
Hints below the input are read after the teacher has already typed, which is too late. Placing hints directly under the label means the guidance is visible before the teacher starts filling in a field.
Audience context moved to cluster headers as micro-badges
The full-width coloured banners were too long and dense to scan quickly. Small "Students see this" and "AI only" pills on the cluster header communicate the same information in a fraction of the space, without interrupting the form flow.
In order / Any order moved below the Learning goals label
The inline pill toggle next to the label competed visually with the label text. Placing it below as a labelled "Goal order" control makes it a clear, deliberate choice rather than a decoration.
Cluster headers made visible with uppercase labels
Cluster headers were invisible horizontal rules (font-size: 0). Making them visible small-caps text creates scannable section breaks within each step card, so teachers can jump to the group they need without reading everything.
Em dashes removed from all visible UI text
Em dashes read as typographic flourish rather than plain instruction. Replaced with full stops, commas, and colons throughout hints, tooltips, and toggle labels.
v28 20 Apr 2026
Character toggle border-radius fixed — gray fill no longer bleeds past card corners
The .buddy-customise-toggle had border-radius: 0 when closed inside the character card, letting its gray background visibly overflow the card's rounded bottom corners. Fixed: closed toggle inherits the card's bottom radius; open toggle stays squared off.
Character roles renamed back to Teacher / Friend / Custom
Sifu and Kaki are culturally resonant but were confusing to new teachers unfamiliar with the terms. Teacher, Friend, and Custom are immediately self-explanatory and match the language used elsewhere in the product.
All text-field placeholders made language-agnostic
Placeholders previously swapped to Chinese / Malay / Tamil examples when the language selector changed — making them useless as guides for the teacher filling the form. One set of English-language instructional placeholders now works across all three MTL contexts. Vocab suggestions still update per language.
Form decluttered — section subtitles removed, spacing tightened, redundant hints pruned
Section subtitles restated what the section title already said. Buddy-name and quirks hints were obvious from the field label and placeholder. Reducing section-card padding, field-cluster margin, and f-group spacing cuts perceived form length without removing any fields.
Back button changed to outline secondary style
The ghost style (invisible border, muted text) was too light — teachers missed it or dismissed it as inactive. Outline secondary gives it a visible border and ink-coloured label consistent with shadcn/ui's secondary button pattern.
v27 19 Apr 2026
"Key vocabulary" renamed to "Extra vocabulary"
Maps to language_task.vocab_list[] — these are words beyond the textbook unit vocab, chosen by the teacher for the specific scenario. "Extra" better signals they are supplementary rather than foundational.
Audience callout badges added (Students see this / Only the AI reads this)
Teachers were unsure which fields appeared in the student interface and which were invisible prompt instructions. Colour-coded callout strips at the top of each section now make this explicit at a glance — green for student-visible, purple for AI-only.
Avatar changed from image grid to scenario dropdown
Named scenario options (Otter at school, Otter at home, Otter at the market…) are easier to scan and understand than a grid of small icons. The dropdown also scales more cleanly as new scenarios are added.
Voice preview button added alongside voice selector
Teachers have no way to know what "Coral" or "Alloy" sound like without hearing them. The Preview button plays a short sample in the target language using the selected voice — so the choice is based on what students actually hear.
Gender field reduced to Female / Male (Neutral removed)
Neutral was the default but produced ambiguous language in practice. All active Buddy personas are gendered; Neutral is not used. Removing it avoids a default that has no real effect and may confuse teachers.
Character names changed to Sifu and Kaki
"Teacher" and "Friend" were functional labels, not character names. Sifu and Kaki are culturally resonant Singapore terms — Sifu connotes a respected guide; Kaki means buddy or mate. More memorable and age-appropriate for secondary students.
Name + Gender and Avatar + Voice fields laid out side-by-side
Both pairs are short, related fields — stacking them vertically wasted space and made the form feel longer than it is. Side-by-side layout reduces perceived form length and groups related choices naturally.
Completion criteria removed
This will be derived automatically from learning goals on the backend, eliminating a field teachers should not need to fill manually.
All inline hints made more concise; proximity tightened to 4 px
Longer hint text increased form weight without helping more. Shorter, direct hints are faster to read and visually closer to their field — reinforcing the rule of proximity so teachers scan the form rather than read it.
v26 19 Apr 2026
Learning goals redesigned — clearer card per goal, renamed sub-fields
"Sentence structure" → "Sentence hint" and "Example student response" → "Model answer" are more self-explanatory. Each goal is now a proper card with labelled fields and contextual hints, replacing the cramped numbered row format. Objective mode moved inline with the label as a compact pill toggle.
Character selection and Character details unified into one visual card
Role cards and the Customise Buddy panel were visually disconnected — teachers couldn't tell they were related. Wrapping both in a single bordered card makes the hierarchy immediately clear: pick a character at top, see and adjust its details below.
"Buddy personality" renamed to "Select character"
More intuitive for teachers — "personality" is abstract; "character" matches how students experience it (a named persona).
Speaking phase and Conversation length removed
Speaking phase was developer-facing context; Conversation length had no clear teacher-facing value at mission creation time. Removing both reduces form length without losing meaningful teacher control.
All tooltips and hints rewritten in plain language
Previous copy used terms like "calibrates", "knowledge base", "proficiency band default", "class override" — jargon teachers don't need to know. New copy focuses on what each field does for students in the room.
Language mix label simplified (removed "class override")
The parenthetical was a system-architecture note, not useful to teachers. Simplified to just "Language mix".
Key vocabulary: clearer guidance for beyond-textbook words
Updated placeholder and hint now explicitly tell teachers to think beyond the textbook unit — words specific to the scenario students are likely to need.
v25 19 Apr 2026
Step 2 more options: Guiding tip + Mission context merged into "Other context"
Too many small optional fields increased cognitive load without proportionate value. A single free-text "Other context" field — with rich placeholder guidance — covers lesson background, Buddy nudges, success signals, and textbook references without forcing teachers into separate boxes.
Step 3: Buddy roles reduced from 4 to 3 (Teacher / Friend / Custom)
Challenger and Character were low-usage presets that added choice without enough differentiation. Teacher and Friend cover the main MOE classroom dynamics; Custom lets specialist teachers go fully bespoke.
Step 3: Customise Buddy panel auto-expands on role selection; pre-fills Teacher/Friend defaults
Preset values (name, personality, quirks, avatar, voice) are now shown immediately on role select — set by the school's LangBuddy specialist. Teachers see the defaults before deciding whether to override, rather than discovering them behind a collapsed toggle. Custom starts blank.
Tooltips and dropdowns no longer clipped within containers
Removed overflow:hidden from .pill-group and .buddy-customise — the main culprits. Border-radius is now applied directly to first/last pill children and to the toggle/body elements, so the visual rounding is preserved without clipping floated descendants.
v24 19 Apr 2026
Step 1: Speaking phase selector (看后说 / 听后说 / Standalone)
Maps to the Situation field in the coach template — tells the Buddy what the student just experienced (read, listened, or nothing) so it can open the conversation in the right context.
Step 2: "Learning focus" field removed
Had no basis in the prompt spec — mission_template.json has task_name (→ "The task") and learning_objectives[], but no standalone learning focus field. Removing it eliminates a confusing duplicate of the task description.
Step 2: Objective rows now include an example sub-field
Maps to learning_objectives[].example in the mission template — lets teachers give the Buddy a concrete model response to benchmark student output against.
Step 2: "Sentence patterns" replaced by "Guiding tip"
Sentence structure now lives per-objective (learning_objectives[].structure). A standalone patterns textarea had no spec backing; "Guiding tip" maps correctly to language_task.tip — optional teacher nudges for the Buddy's coaching style.
