Changelog
Recent mission creation updates.
Current 11 Apr 2026 Latest
The top intro now invites teachers into what they can create for students
Instead of explaining the product mechanically, the intro now frames missions as something teachers can shape to help students speak more independently and use the language in conversation.
"About this mission" is now a simpler setup prompt
Moving the product explanation out of Step 1 keeps the first section lighter and makes it easier to focus on the mission basics straight away.
Placeholders are back to a more example-led, teacher-guiding style across Chinese, Malay, and Tamil
The earlier rewrite became too flat and generic. Richer examples make it clearer how to write a fun, classroom-ready mission while keeping the structure simple.
"Design the mission" now stays focused on the mission itself, not Buddy setup
The planning section now talks about the scenario, student speaking goal, and language support, while Buddy-specific guidance is kept in the later Buddy step where it belongs.
Banding simplified to G1, G2, G3, and Higher MTL, with Step 1 kept focused on the mission itself
Higher MTL belongs in the banding, not as a separate language. Removing extra class-context fields from Step 1 also keeps the opening setup lighter and easier to scan.
v16 11 Apr 2026
Required fields marked with * — Mission name, Description, Learning focus, The task
No consistent signal for required vs optional fields made the form harder to scan. Asterisk is universal and adds zero words for optional fields.
"Optional add-ons" subtitle removed from Key vocabulary; all "Optional" inline labels removed
Absence of * already signals optional — the extra label was redundant and added visual noise.
Section subtitles and field hints rewritten to be shorter and more action-oriented
Previous copy was generic and too long — didn't guide or inspire. Short, specific copy is faster to read and feels more purposeful.
v15 11 Apr 2026
Create Mission redesigned as a step-based flow
Single-page scroll was cognitively heavy — teachers couldn't gauge progress or know what was left. One section at a time reduces overload and makes the task feel manageable.
Previous / Next controls + free step-jumping via nav
Teachers need to move non-linearly — jumping back to fix a title mid-flow shouldn't require scrolling through the whole form.
Review state before publish
Gives teachers one final check across all steps before committing — reduces publish errors and builds confidence in the flow.
Step 4 (Assign) visually consistent with other steps
Previously used a different card style — unified so each step feels like part of the same flow.
v14 11 Apr 2026
Audience selector rewritten to feel more teacher-first
The goal was to make the setup sound like how teachers actually think when choosing the student group for a mission.
Knowledge base syncs to selected group
KB should always match the student group — keeping them out of sync invites errors.
Textbook topics nested under MTL toggle; Knowledge base moved above Key vocabulary
Topics only exist within the curriculum — nesting shows the dependency. Structural context should come before teacher add-ons.
Topic selector: scrollable checklist with search + selected tags
Chip grid breaks above ~12 items. Checklist scales to 50+ and search makes long lists fast to navigate.
Key vocabulary → "Optional add-ons" · Language balance → "Language mix"
"Optional add-ons" clarifies vocab is supplemental, not the base. "Mix" replaces "balance" — balance implies 50/50, mix reflects a sliding blend.
v13 4 Apr 2026 Baseline
Starting point. Static knowledge base, Audience label, Key vocabulary above Knowledge base.
