For discussion
How might we get teachers to use CoVAA and keep coming back?
Draft principles from the survey. React to each — what lands, what needs changing?
01
CoVAA has to feel like a safe mentor, not an observer with a clipboard.
The moment a teacher feels CoVAA is grading them rather than helping them, they disengage. That's the thing we most cannot afford.
"Feels like judging them" — 8/9 both groups · "Safe space to reflect" and "Help you improve" tied as top desired message (5/9 each)
02
Teachers return to things they trust. CoVAA has to earn both warmth and credibility.
Supportive gets teachers through the door. Credible keeps them coming back. CoVAA needs to do both — neither alone is enough to build a habit.
Supportive 8/9 · Credible 7/9 · both groups picked both as top tone words
03
The moment it feels like extra work, it's gone.
Teachers aren't looking for another tool to manage. CoVAA needs to feel lighter than not using it — or it becomes something people close without guilt.
"Feels like extra admin" — 8/9 · "Hard to use" — 7/9 · "Worth returning to" tied #1 fix priority (5/9)
04
What CoVAA does for a teacher should be obvious in the first 10 seconds.
Teachers who can't immediately see the value won't give it a second chance. The product has to answer "what's in it for me?" before anyone has to ask.
"Support easier to understand" tied #1 fix at 5/9 · "Clearer what it's for" and "Clearer how it's different" also flagged
Decide today
05
CoVAA needs to feel both approachable to teachers and credible to the system. Those aren't opposites — but we haven't pinned down what that balance looks like.
The product team is thinking from a teacher's experience — warm, safe, easy. AST is thinking from an institutional lens — it needs to carry weight. CoVAA has to serve both. The question is where it leans when those two pull in different directions.
2.3 point gap on warm↔professional scale · Product team avg 5.0/6 · AST avg 2.7/6 · AST unanimously picked "Professional" as desired tone (3/3)
